3D Printed Bridge

Sept. 2021 - Nov. 2021

I was selected to represent my high school in the NJIT 3D Printed Bridge Competition. The goal of the competition was to design an aesthetically-pleasing, lightweight, and stiff bridge that will take the least amount of print time and assembly time.



Different views of designed bridge



Throughout our research and iterative design process, we made several decisions. We created sections for even load distribution to avoid vulnerable fault lines and reduce support material. We also decided to use tenon and mortise joints that were inspired by woodworking to lower human error. Finally, we decided to have filleted edges and corners for additional support and to decrease stress concentration.

We also performed force calculations with our design using method of sections and finding forces in the center X truss using equilibrium equations. We determined load distribution by calculating zero force members then solving.

We presented our bridge and design to a panel of professional engineering judges and won first place in multiple categories.
